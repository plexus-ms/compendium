The Plexus Brand Kit v1.0

This page is the definitive brand reference, folded into one document and set in its own system: IBM Plex Mono and IBM Plex Sans, ink on cool paper, nerve ochre as the event color. The kit follows the doctrine it dresses: each rule below was decided once, is stated once, and propagates everywhere the brand appears.

Section 1 · The mark

KIT-1

The Crossing

Two S-curved strands crossing once, the rising strand passing over — the smallest unit of a braid, and the ✕ in ple✕us. It passes the second-reader test in one sentence: “two strands crossing once, one passing over the other.”

Clearspace & minimum sizes

construction — curvature: S-curve (A) · over: rising strand · gap: 0.5× stroke per side, cut geometrically into the under strand — the assets work on any ground · masters: stroke 7 ≥24 px, stroke 9 <24 px

Section 2 · The wordmark

KIT-2

ple✕us — the mark is the letter

The wordmark is IBM Plex Mono Bold, lowercase, with the crossing standing in for the x. The ✕ runs heavier than the letters on purpose — ~1.2× the bold stem weight (0.135 em against Plex's 0.11 em stems) — so it reads as the mark it is. Its total advance equals exactly one mono cell: it occupies the grid like the letter it replaces.

Section 3 · The braid band

KIT-3

The crossing, repeated

Zoom out and the crossing extends into the braid — the system's divider and header motif for the site and the compendium. Horizontal only, same stroke grammar as the mark, two-tone follows the same color rule. It is decoration with a pedigree: the mark's own geometry, propagated.

Section 4 · Typography

KIT-4

IBM Plex Mono + IBM Plex Sans

One superfamily, one skeleton — mono carries the voice (headlines, labels, wordmark, code), sans carries the reading (prose at a 65-character measure). Serif and condensed cuts remain in reserve for future registers without a new decision.

Section 7 · Hard rules

  • RULE-1Don't straighten the crossing. A straight X is a close button. The S-curve is the etymology.
  • RULE-2Don't rotate it. The rising diagonal is the point.
  • RULE-3Don't close the gap. The over-under is the entire concept. The gap is cut into the under strand's geometry — never painted over in a ground color.
  • RULE-4Yellow is an event. If ochre appears more than once per viewport, something is being shouted that should be said.
  • RULE-5The band runs horizontal only, and the ✕ never appears in a word other than the name.
